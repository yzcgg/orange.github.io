passage 274

The novel Georges, published under Alexander Dumas’name, may actually have been written by Felicien Mallefille. A comparison of Georges with well-known Dumas novels such as The Count of Monte Cristo suggests that Dumas had little to do with Georges. For example, Georges’ characters are tiresomely earnest, while those from Dumas’ wel-known novels crack jokes and utter colorful oaths. Meanwhile, in a technical sense, Georges is well-written, while The Count of Monte Cristo, with all its verve and sparkle, is full of redundancies, repetitions, and non sequiturs. As Umberto Eco discovered while attempting to translate The Count of Monte Cristo, the charm of Dumas’ novel and its “narrative wisdom”are inseparable from its “linguistically sludgy and gasping” prose.

解析：小说乔治（Georges），以D的名字出版，实际上可能是M写的。乔治这本小说与著名的D小说的比较表明，D与乔治没有任何关系。举例来说，乔治的人物真诚而又令人生厌，而D著名小说中那些人物则爱开玩笑。同时，从技术上讲，乔治写的很完美，而Count of Monte Cristo在充满活力和光芒的同时，到处都是重复和不拘一格。正如翁贝托·艾考（Umberto Eco）在尝试翻译Count of Monte Cristo时所发现的那样，D小说的魅力和其“叙事智慧”与它的“语言上的污秽和大喘气”密不可分。

  • tiresome adj.讨厌的,烦人的
  • earnest adj.认真的,真诚的
  • crack a joke 开玩笑,说笑话
  • utter adj.完全的,彻底的 v.出生,说,讲
  • oath n.宣誓,誓言
  • verve n.精力,激情,热情
  • sparkle v.闪烁 n.活力
  • non sequitur n.不合逻辑的推论
  • inseparable adj.不可分开的
  • sludgy adj.泥泞的

1.The author of the passage would be most likely to agree with which of the following statements about Dumas as a novelist?

A. His use of non sequiturs detracts from the stylistic verve of his writing.

  • detract v.贬低,减损 而 verve 和 non sequitur 都是形容 Dumas 这个人的作品的
  • stylistic adj.体裁上的,格式上的

B. His use of repetition has been overrated as effective novelistic technique.

  • overrate v.高估,对...评价过高 无中生有

C.His reliance on humor results in one-dimensional characters.

也是属于纯扯淡类选项

D.His novels, while technically well written, have predictable plots.

George 不是 D 的novel, George 是 well written , D 应该就不能说 well written. 而且后面还说了 D 的作品有很多重复. 再而且, predictable plots 可预测的情节也是扯淡.

E.His writing displays narrative skill despite certain technical flaws.

文中最后一句. 这个选项的描述确实没毛病.

答案：E
解析：根据Dumas as a novelist定位，文中在两个地方提到了D的小说特征。those from Dumas’ well-known novels crack jokes and utter colorful oaths. Meanwhile, in a technical sense, …with all its verve and sparkle, is full of redundancies, repetitions, and non sequiturs. E选项中说的narrative skill 对应“verve and sparkle”，certain technical flaws 对应 “ full of redundancies, repetitions, and non sequiturs”。
A错：distract跟原文的意思正好相反。
B错：overrated文中没有提到。
C错：无中生有。
D错：predictable plots无中生有。

2.According to the passage, which of the following is true regarding the novel Georges?
A. lts authorship was widely questioned soon after its publication.

widely 没体现, 只是作者一个人这样认为, 并没有提到当时社会上大众是怎么看的.

B. Its characters are less interesting than those in most Dumas’ novels.

对的. 因为提到Duma的一个作品里有joke, 也说George 是 tiresome

C. lt is marred by redundancies, repetitions,and non sequiturs.

  • mar v.破坏,损害 后面这三个形容词都是形容D的作品的.

D. lt presents unusual problems for translators.

翻译家翻译的是D的作品, 不是George

E. It has often been compared favorably to The Count of Monte Cristo

去掉favorably, 因为adv.一般不会影响主要意思,
只是作者比较了两部作品, 并不能说社会大众经常把两部作品相互比较.

答案：B
解析：根据关键词定位回原文，B选项“Its characters are less interesting than those in most Dumas novels.”是文中“Georges’ characters are tiresomely earnest, while those from Dumas’ well-known novels crack jokes and utter colorful oaths. ”的同义改写。
A错：无中生有。
C错：redundancies, repetitions, and non sequiturs是D小说的特征。
D错：无中生有。
E错：无中生有。

3.The quotation from Umberto Eco most directly serves to
A. underscore an apparent inconsistency
B. introduce a new explanation
C. reevaluate the critical status of a work
D. point out a contradiction in a critical stance
E. enumerate certain challenges of translation

  • enumerate v.列举,枚举

这个题基本上五个选项翻译出来了就是排除法了.
答案：A
解析：定位到文章最后一句话，注意这里的as是正如的意思，明显是顺承证明上文的观点，所以答案选A选项。

passage 276

While studying different-sized ant colonies, Cole discovered that when there were only a few ants in the space provided, each individual exhibited a chaotic pattern of activity and rest. As the density of the colony increased by the addition of more ants, Cole observed a sudden transition to dynamic order: patterns of activity and rest over the colony as a whole suddenly changed from chaotic to rhythmic. Why should the density of ants play an apparently crucial role in the transition from chaotic to ordered behavior?Ants interact with one another,and an active ant encountering an inactive one will stimulate the latter into movement. At low densities there are few encounters, but at higher densities activity can spread like a contagion through the colony.

Consider each of the choices separately and select all that apply.
1.According to the passage, which of the following statements about ant interactions is true?
A.The interaction between active and inactive ants leads to a change in the inactive ant’s behavior.
B. When the density of an ant colony is low, there are relatively few interactions between ants.
C. When there are many ants in an ant colony, the perceived lack of space often leads ants to inactivity.

2.In the context in which it appears,”ordered”most nearly means
A.prepared
B. prearranged
C.regular
D. peaceful
E.restricted

简单题, 不过多的看了.
在研究不同大小的蚁群时，C发现当提供的空间中只有几只蚂蚁时，每个蚂蚁都表现出混乱的活动和休息模式。随着添加更多蚂蚁而增加了colony的密度，C观察到了一个突然的转变，转向了秩序：整个colony的活动和休息模式突然从混乱变为有节奏。为什么蚂蚁的密度在从混沌行为向有序行为的转变中起着至关重要的作用？蚂蚁彼此互动，而一只活动的蚂蚁遇到不活动的蚂蚁会刺激后者运动。在低密度时，蚂蚁之间很少遇到，但在高密度时，活动可以像传染病一样在colony传播。

1.答案：AB
解析：多选题。
根据ant interactions定位，Ants interact with one another, and an active ant encountering an inactive one will stimulate the latter into movement. At low densities there are few encounters, but at higher densities activity can spread like a contagion through the colony. 前面一句对应A，后面一句对应B。
C错，无中生有。
2.答案：C
解析：词汇题。
根据上下文的意思，这里的ordered与chaotic取反，C选项regular最符合。

passage 245

Native American music is unique in the world in having almost no string instruments. The fretless,single-string violin of Mexico’s Seri lndians is therefore often remarked on in studies of NativeAmerican music.Most scholars assume that the Seri violin descended from the European violin.The Spanish introduced a variety of string instruments to Mexico following the conquest (1519),and these subsequently spread widely in many forms. However, Helen Roberts questions other scholars’arguments that Mexican string instruments could not have preconquest origins simply because they are not mentioned by early Spanish sources and do not appear in indigenous Mexican illustrations.Roberts argues that because of their low volume, string instruments would not have been used in loud group contexts such as those depicted in Mexican manuscripts.

美洲原住民的音乐在世界上是独一无二的，几乎没有弦乐器。因此，墨西哥塞里印第安人的无音单弦小提琴在研究美洲原住民音乐时经常被提及。大多数学者认为塞里小提琴起源于欧洲小提琴。西班牙人在征服墨西哥之后(1519年)向墨西哥引进了各种弦乐器，这些乐器随后以多种形式广泛传播。然而，海伦·罗伯茨质疑其他学者的观点，即墨西哥弦乐器不可能起源于被征服前，仅仅是因为早期西班牙文献中没有提到它们，也没有出现在墨西哥土著的插图中。罗伯茨认为，由于弦乐器的音量小，它们不可能被用在吵闹的群体环境中，比如墨西哥手稿中描述的那些。

  • conquest n.征服,占领
  • illustration n.插图
  • be descended from … 是...的后裔

1.In the context in which it appears, “simply” most nearly means
A. artlessly
B. frankly
C.ordinarily
D. slightly
E. just

2.It can be inferred from the passage that the view held by most scholars regarding the origins of the Seri violin is based in part on
A. differences between the Seri violin and European violins
B. the rarity of string instruments among Native American peoples

  • rarity n.罕见

C. the fact that the Seri violin lacks frets and has only a single string

  • fret n.琴格

D.a resemblance between the Seri violin and Spanish string instruments of the sixteenth century
E.the means by which string instruments spread in Mexico following the conquest

答案 E
解析：定位在the spanish introduced…这句话，这个结构应该就是most scholars assume…提出观点，下一句对观点进行原因解释。开头两句是事实，是文章的背景。答案选 E。

3.The passage suggests that in Roberts’view, the Seri violin

B. was probably modeled on string instruments introduced to Mexico by the Spanish
C. was not likely to have been in use prior to the Spanish conquest of Mexico

我这个题目错的原因是没有仔细看完所有选项就选择了.

passage 219

Ecologists had assumed that trees in the consistently warm tropics grew at a slow but steady rate,unvarying from year to year. However, a study at La Selva, Costa Rica, showed that trees grew less in hotter years and more in cooler ones: between 1984 and 2000, dramatic differences occurred in the six species of trees studies, with trees adding twice as much wood in some cooler years as they did in the scorching El Nino year of 1997-1998.Because tree growth is an index of the balance between photosynthesis, in which trees absorb carbon dioxide (C02) from the atmosphere and release oxygen, and respiration, in which the opposite occurs, the La Selva data were the first hint that rapidly rising global temperatures, driven by human-generated emissions of co2, may be pushing tropical forests to release more CO2, thereby intensifying global warming.This raised serious questions about a popular theory that tropical forests act as a sponge, soaking up much of the excess CO2 that humans pump into the atmosphere.The La Selva data are consistent with a model of global CO2 flux developed by Keeling, who concluded that the amount of CO2 taken up in tropical landmasses rose in cooler years and fell in hotter ones,accounting for year-to-year changes in the amount of CO2 that stays in the atmosphere.

  • tropic n.热带地区
  • scorch v.烧焦,烤糊 scorching adj.酷热的
  • photosynthesis n.光合作用
  • respiration n.呼吸
  • soak up 吸收 其实放在语境里能猜出来
  • flux n.不断的变化
  • account for 解释,说明
