test58

  1. Though acknowledging the unstated assumptions that commentators on her paper had identified, the geologist pointed out that such assumptions were legitimate within the context of her research and therefor their existence did not impede her conclusions.

    尽管….,这个geologist指出所有的assumptions都在他的research的上下文的合法范围之内,所以assumption不影响结论.

  • antithetical 对立的,正相反的
  • legitimate 合法的,合理的
  • implausible 难以置信的,不合理的,不像真实的
  1. Penrose is critical of theoretical physicists for engaging in conjecture what is implied by venturing beyond The known data. One of his favorite examples is inflationary cosmology, which, according to him, is treated as an established theory despite a lack of evidence.

    P被物理学家批评,因为他在已知数据之外进行猜测.他最喜欢的例子之一是暴胀宇宙论，在他看来,尽管缺乏证据,但它被视为一个既定的理论.

  • singular 突出的
  • conjecture 猜测
  • venture n.投机 v.敢于去,冒险做...
  1. The author argued that the industrial agricultural system that prevails in the United States is unsustainable and, therefore, anything that_______it should be questioned.
  • streamline v.使...呈流线型; 提高...效率
  • hamper v.阻碍,妨碍
  • encumber v.阻碍,妨碍
  • reinforce v.加强,加固
  • buttress v.支持,支撑
  • prevail v.流行,盛行
  • unsustainable v.不能持续的
  1. Suburbanization arguably ushered in numerous changes in the way many people in the United States lived. the suburbs______a society based on unprecedented consumption and intense privacy.
  • usher v.引导,引领;
  • usher in v.开创,开启
  • validate v.批准,许可;证实
  • affirm v.公开肯定,证实
  • enliven v.使有活力
  • engender v.产生,引起
  • inaugurate v.引进,开创
  1. His lackadaisical approach to this project belies his well-known reputation for______.
  • lackadaisical 无精打采的,没精神的 来自短语lack a day
  • belie v.掩饰
  1. Many of the mid-1980s studies of the interaction between solar wind and the cometary ionosphere represent scientific noise rather than true advances, since the haste in interpreting the data frequently led to______or even erroneous results.
    20世纪80年代中期，许多关于太阳风和彗星电离层相互作用的研究代表了科学上的噪音，而不是真正的进步，因为对数据的匆忙解释经常导致琐碎甚至错误的结果。

    even表示递进,要选一个程度弱于erroneous的

  • erroneous 错误的
  • interpret v.解释
  1. Politicians who invoke the founders of the United States in support of their views seem to imply that the founders consistently concurred in their own views when in reality they were a highly _____ group of thinkers.
  • concur v.同意,意见一致
  • contentious 爱争论的,引发争论的
  • erudite 博学的
  1. When film adaptations of literary classics are lackluster it is often because they display a certain obeisance to the cultural prestige of literature. The good ones, by contrast, succeed through the arrogant assumption that a great novel is not a sacred artifact but rather a lump of interesting material to be shaped according to the filmmaker’s will.

    改编自文学经典的电影表现平平，往往是因为它们对文学的文化威望表现出某种程度的敬意。相比之下，优秀的小说之所以成功，是因为他们傲慢地认为，一部伟大的小说不是一件神圣的神器，而是一堆有趣的材料，可以根据导演的意愿加以塑造

  • hostile 敌意的,敌对的
  • obeisance 敬意,尊敬
  • sacred 神圣的
  • malleable 可锻造的,可塑的
  • generic 一般的,通用的
