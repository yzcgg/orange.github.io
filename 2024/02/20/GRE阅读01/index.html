passage 297

In A Fine Brush on Ivory, his appreciation of novelist Jane Austen, Richard Jenkyns remarks that in Austen scholarship, there are pressure that cause ordinary critical circumspection to break down. Principal among those pressures is the peculiar affection in which the person of Jane Austen is held by many readers. This affection is not altogether explained by admiration for he genius, nor is it entirely a symptom of nostalgia for her orderly, decorous, even mysterious life. Writer has led critics to approach her work in mostly biographical or historical ways, often in defiance of other critical fashions, especially the various formal approaches that have dominated modern literary criticism.

在《象牙上的画笔》一书中，J对小说家简·奥斯汀的评价是这样的：在奥斯汀的学术领域，存在着使普通的文学评论崩溃的压力。在这些压力中，最主要的是很多读者对简·奥斯汀持有的独特情感。这种感情不完全是因为对天才的钦佩，也不完全是对她井然有序、高雅、甚至神秘生活的怀念。作家引导评论家们以传记或历史的方式来看待她的作品，这些常常是对其他文学评论方式的蔑视，尤其是对现代文学批评中占主导地位的各种形式方法的蔑视。

  • 如何知道一串单词是一本书的名字?

  • ivory n.象牙
  • appreciate v.欣赏
  • principal adj.最重要的,主要的 n.大学校长
  • affection n.喜爱,感情
  • altogether adv.完全地
  • symptom n.症状
  • defiance n.违抗,蔑视

Consider each of the choices separately and select all that apply.
1.According to the passage, critics approach Jane Austen’s work in biographical or historical ways for which of the following reasons?
A.They find her work particularly well suited to biographical and historical readings.
B.They are unsatisfied with the formal approaches that have dominated modern literary criticism.
C.They feel the need to know the baffling writer personally.

  • baffle v.使困惑; adj.令人困惑的 其实这个单词去掉不影响句子意思.

2.In the context in which it appears, “appreciation of”most nearly means
A. gratitude toward 感激之情, 感谢
B. awareness of
C.appraisal of appraisal 评估,评价
D.esteem for esteem 尊重,敬重;把...看作,认为
E. grasp of

解析：词汇题。
在文章语境中，appreciation是“评价”之意，与C符合。

Some studies have shown that red-backed salamanders (RBS) are scarce in areas with acidic soils and that those present in such conditions have smaller-than-average bodies.Explanations have included the possibility that young RBS are adversely affected by acidic soil,that adult RBS can sense and may avoid acidic soil conditions,or that loss of RBS prey populations due to acidic soil could result in reduced RBS populations. Yet researchers found fairly high densities of large- bodied RBS at Lake Claire Watershed, where soil conditions are acidic.
One hypothesis is that intraspecific geographical variation in acidity tolerance (ie. local adaptation to an acidic environment) could exist for RBS.Previous studies show
of some salamander species to acidity.

1.Which of the following can be inferred about the studies mentioned in the highlighted portion n.一部分 of the passage?

A.They may have undercounted RBS in their study areas due to faulty methodology.

他们可能因为错误的研究方法而把RBS的数量统计少了.

B.They focused less on adult RBS than on RBS in the early stages of their development.

C.They found evidence that acidic conditions adversely affect the prey on which RBS depend.

  • prey n.猎物,v.捕食

D.They drew unsubstantiated conclusions about the possible adverse effects of acidic conditions on RBS.

第一句只是说了一种现象, 不能由一个现象infer出一种结论.

E.They were conducted in areas where the soil conditions were similar to those at Lake ClaireWatershed.

答案：E
解析：推断题。
第一句讨论的是酸性土壤的rbs的生长情况，E正确，对应“Lake Claire Watershed, where soil conditions are acidic…”。
A错，faulty methodology无中生有。
B错，无中生有。
C错，found evidence that acidic conditions adversely affect the prey 无中生有。
D错，unsubstantiated conclusions逻辑错误。作者并未下定论。

2.The primary purpose of the passage is to
A. cite evidence that appears to contradict a widely held assumption

  • cite v.引用

B.present contrasting interpretations of a particular research finding
C.explain how a particular environmental change can produce diverse outcomes
D.note an unexpected finding and summarize new possible explanation for it
E. identify a correlation and explain why one hypothesis about it is more persuasive than others

答案：D
解析：主旨题。
文章的结构是，一开始提出一种发现，然后提供解释。转折后提出新的发现，随后提供对于新发现的解释。D最符合。
A错，文章不只是cite evidence。
B错，文章提出两种findings。而不是针对一种finding的两种解释。
C错，无中生有。
E错，more persuasive 无中生有。

For Temnothorax ants in search of suitable rock crevices for new nest sites, size is an important consideration, and certain behaviors suggest how scouts might compare the size of several sites.After entering a crevice, scouts invariably exit and reenter.During their first inspection, they mark their route with their personal pheromones.(Experiments have shown that workers can distinguish their own pheromones from those of nest mates.) Some researchers believe that these ants are counting the frequency of intersections between their two exploratory trails. The smaller the area,the more likely they are to cross a spot twice.The observation that Temnothorax scouts always pave out a path of the same length on their first visit to sites strongly supports this hypothesis.

对于寻找合适的岩石裂缝作为新巢穴的Temnothorax蚂蚁来说，大小是一个重要的考虑因素，某些行为表明侦察兵可能会比较几个地点的大小。在进入裂缝后，侦察兵总是退出并重新进入。在第一次检查时，它们用自己的信息素标记路线。(实验表明，工蜂可以将自己的信息素与巢穴同伴的信息素区分开来。)一些研究人员认为，这些蚂蚁正在计算它们两条探索路径之间相交的频率。区域越小，它们越有可能两次穿过一个点。Temnothorax侦察兵总是在他们第一次访问地点时开辟出相同长度的路径，这一观察结果有力地支持了这一假设。

1.In light of the claim made by the “researchers”, it can be inferred that ants’ ability to distinguish their own pheromones from these of nest mates is important primarily because
A. ants would otherwise be unable to tell whether potential mating sites were occupied by related colonies.
B.the ability aids in the assignment of specific tracks such as scouting for new nests, among the various members of a nest.
C.ants who could not distinguish their own personal pheromones would be unable to tell if they had previously embraced a site
D.pheromone differentiation is essential to account evaluation of potential nesting sites

这个能力对描述可能的巢穴的评估(evaluation)很重要

E. pheromone differentiation is essential to ants’ability to evaluate the size of crevices before entering them

这个能力对蚂蚁在进入缝隙之前评估缝隙的大小很重要

答案：C
解析：本题问的是researchers的观点，回原文定位至第四句。该句表明，研究人员认为这些蚂蚁会计算它们两次探索路径之间相交的频率。基于此，可知这些蚂蚁能够区分自己与其他成员间的激素很重要，因为如果它们不能区分的话，它们就不知道自己曾经是否走过这条路，从而就没办法去计算两次路径的相交频率，对应C选项。
A选项错，whether从句内容无中生有；
B选项错，assignment of specific tracks无中生有；
D、E选项中，be essential to均找不到对应。
