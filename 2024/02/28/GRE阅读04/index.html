section 122

There have long been dead zones—water too low in oxygen to sustain most forms of life—in the Gulf of Mexico, which receives the waters of the Mississippi River. Scientists studied sediment cores from areas where the gulf’s most recent dead zone occurred. The scientists dated the sediment and counted species of foraminifera (marine protozoans) in the sediment; these species thrive in low-oxygen waters. As far back as 1823, the foraminifera thrived especially during Mississippi River flood years (during which nutrients levels increase), suggesting that nutrients in floodwaters can trigger low-oxygen water. The foraminifera in the core samples were most abundant after 1950, when farmers began using some fertilizer, which is rich in nutrients. Researchers believe that increased use of fertilizer leads to more-extreme dead zones.

解析：长期以来，墨西哥湾一直有死亡地带（水的含氧量太低，无法维持大多数生命形式），这里接收密西西比河的水域。科学家研究了海湾最近死亡区发生地区的沉积物岩心。科学家们测量了沉积物的年代，计算了沉积物中有孔虫（海洋原生动物）的种类。这些物种在低氧水域中茁壮成长。早在1823年，有孔虫就在密西西比河洪水期（营养水平增加期间）蓬勃发展，表明洪水中的营养物质可以引发低氧水。1950年后，当农民开始使用富含营养的肥料时，核心样本中的有孔虫最为丰富。研究人员认为，增加化肥的使用会导致更加极端的死区。

section 123

Biologist know that some marine algae can create clouds by producing the gas dimethylsulphide (DMS), which reacts with oxygen in air above the sea to form solid particles. These particles provide a surface on which water vapor can condense to form clouds. Lovelock contends that this process is part of global climatic-control system. According to Lovelock, Earth acts like a super organism, with all its biological and physical systems cooperating to keep it healthy. He hypothesized that warmer conditions increase algal activity and DMS output, seeding more clouds, which cool the planet by blocking out the Sun. Then, as the climate cools, algal activity and DMS level decrease and the cycle continues. In response to biologists who question how organisms presumably working for their own selfish ends could have evolved to behave in a way that benefits not only the planet but the organisms as well, cooling benefits the algae, which remain at the ocean surface, because it allows the cooled upper layers of the ocean to sink, and then the circulating water carries nutrients upward from the depths below. Algae may also benefit from nitrogen raining down from clouds they have helped to form.

解析：生物学家知道，一些海藻可以通过产生气体二甲基硫化物（DMS）产生云，DMS与海洋上空的氧气反应形成固体颗粒。这些颗粒提供了一个表面，水蒸气可以凝结在该表面上形成云。L认为这个过程是全球气候控制系统的一部分。根据L的说法，地球就像一个超级有机体，其所有的生物和物理系统都在合作以保持健康。他假设温暖的条件会增加藻类活动和DMS输出，播种更多的云，通过阻挡太阳来冷却地球。然后，随着气候变冷，藻类活动和DMS水平降低，循环继续。为了回应生物学家的质疑（他们质疑有机体为自己的自私目的而工作的方式是如何演变成一种不仅有益于地球而且有益于生物的方式），L指出，冷却有益于留在海洋表面的藻类，因为它允许冷却的海洋上层下沉，然后循环水从下面的深处向上运送养分。藻类也可能受益于从它们帮助形成的云层中下来的氮气。
